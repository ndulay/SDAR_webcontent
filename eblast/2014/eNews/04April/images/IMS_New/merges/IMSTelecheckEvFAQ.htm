1.   What is the evaluation process? The Evaluation Process assigns points for information relevant to the transaction. For example, points
  are assigned for the amount of your check, the type of merchant, your history with TeleCheck, and so on. All of the points are added
  together to evaluate your purchase. The procedure is an automated evaluation tool used to evaluate both transactional AND individual
  risk as it relates to checking activity.
2.   Is this type of evaluation procedure a new concept? No, companies have been using this type of evaluation method for over 40 years
  to process consumer loans, credit card applications, and tax returns.
3.   How does the evaluation process work? All of TeleCheck's daily check transactions are stored in positive and negative databases and
  are used to evaluate factors contributing to returned checks. The evaluation process looks at the type of merchant, amount of your
  check, your history in TeleCheck's positive and negative databases, and so on.
4.   Is the decision to approve my check made by a machine? Computers are used to process information according to decision
  algorithms developed based upon historical experience.
5.   How is an evaluation process developed? Most evaluation processes are unique because they are based on a company's individual
  experiences. To develop a system, a company will select a sample of transactions and analyze it statistically to identify which
  characteristics of those transactions could be used to demonstrate check risk. Then, again using statistical methods, a company will
  weigh each of these factors based on how well each predicts which transactions would be a good check risk.
6.   How valid is the evaluation process? With such evaluation processes, companies are able to evaluate millions of authorizations
  consistently and impartially on many different characteristics. But these processes must be based on large enough numbers of recent
  authorizations to make them statistically valid. TeleCheck's evaluation processes are built using data elements from hundreds of
  thousands of recent check authorizations. Although you think that such a system is arbitrary or impersonal, a properly developed
  evaluation process can make decisions faster and more accurately than an individual can.
7.   How does TeleCheck decide whether to accept or decline a transaction? The results of the evaluation process will help TeleCheck decide
  whether or not to accept the check presented. If the transaction evaluation is too low we will unfortunately have to decline the transaction.
8.   Will TeleCheck provide details of a transaction evaluation? TeleCheck does not provide this information in its entirety
  because it would make our system less effective and secure. The security of our system is key to preserving checks
  as a valid option of payment at the point of sale. For this reason, scores, components and other details of the evaluation
  process are confidential.
9.   If TeleCheck declines to process my Internet check transaction, does this mean the merchant will not accept my check?
  Not necessarily. Even if TeleCheck declines to process your check transaction over the Internet, the merchant
  may be willing to accept a physical check sent directly to them. See the merchant's web site or contact them
  via the methods shown on their web site for more information.
