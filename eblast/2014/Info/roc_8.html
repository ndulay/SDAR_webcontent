San Diego's Proposed Climate Action Plan Could Freeze Local Home Sales.

San Diego's City Council has proposed a Climate Action Plan that would require local home sellers to make mandated environmental home improvements prior to sale. This hot button issue has ignited our local REALTOR® community because of its potential for harming the local housing market. SDAR President Leslie Kilpatrick recently wrote an opinion piece in the Union Tribune about the proposed climate action plan and the effect it would have on San Diego's homeowners and economy.

C.A.R. Sponsored Bill Gains State Senate Approval

 San Diego's Assemblyman Brian Maienschein's bill AB 2430 has made it to the Governor's desk. The bill is an amendment to The Davis-Stirling Common Interest Development Act which requires homeowners associations to provide homebuyers specific governance, finance, and policy documents. The amendment would allow associations to collect a reasonable fee from the seller for the actual cost of providing these documents, or require the seller to provide their current documents to the buyer free of charge.

HUD to Allow Dual Agency Agreements in FHA Pre-foreclosure Transactions

On Friday, July 14, 2014, the US Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) issued Mortgagee Letter 2014-15 allowing dual agency agreements in FHA pre-foreclosure transactions. Dual agency includes transactions in which two agents are working for the same broker and one agent represents the seller and the other agent represents the buyer.
