Dear Volunteer,

As you all know many throughout the country are mourning the tragedy that occurred in Newtown, Connecticut which took the young lives of 20 children and 6 teachers. As an association we too mourn the this tragedy and our hearts and sympathy feel for the families during this time of loss. In an effort to do our part, our association has worked quickly to assemble a place for San Diego to mourn. As a result we have decided to step forward and do our part. In the association's front yard we have 20 smaller trees, one for each child, and 6 large trees, one for each teacher, for the community to gather today at 5:30 for a tree lighting ceremony and to stop by anytime between today and Christmas to bring an ornament, card, letter, stuffed animal or anything else to help express their sorrow and sympathy. Additionally, the association has provided ornament and cards for anyone to stop by through Christmas to place on a tree for the young victims.

So far the outpouring has been enormous and huge. Several news and TV networks have been stopping by to help ensure all of San Diego knows about the Christmas Tree memorial we have created. Several community partners have stepped forward to help. The Pinery Christmas Trees and Home Depot Mira Mesa, have generously donated over $1,000 of trees, 2,000 lights, and other supplies for the memorial, and we have had many members of the community that have already come by to visit the memorial. One mother with her children had an ornament made for each child. The reaction from the community is huge.

We ask you to let your agents know about the memorial and encourage them to stop by today at 5:30 for the lighting ceremony and official launch of the tree memorial. Additionally we encourage everyone and anyone to stop by anytime from today through Christmas to place an ornament on the tree, or write a card to express your sympathy.

We, like many of you are a parent and we can only image the pain and tremendous loss the parents of those 20 children are experiencing. We also know the teachers who teach our children and appreciate the love and dedication to educate and care for our children. The teachers from Sandy Hook Elementary are truly heroes to be remembered.

Thank you in advance for you participation and we at SDAR wish everyone a wonderful Holiday Season with your families.

Thank you,


Donna Sanfilippo
2012 President

Mike Mercurio
SDAR Chief Executive Officer
